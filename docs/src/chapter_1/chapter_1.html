### 1.6 Robots of the future ##### Artificial Intelligence **What is Artificial Intelligence?** * **Artificial Intelligence** , or **AI**, is when computers or machines are made to think and learn like humans. * It helps them do things that usually need human brains: like recognizing voices, understanding pictures, and making decisions. * To be called an AI, a computer needs to be taught to think like a human. This process is called **machine learning**.
### 1.6 Robots of the future ##### Artificial Intelligence **How Does AI Learn? -- Machine Learning** * Most AI learn from lots of information: * We show it a huge number of examples, and it figures out patterns. * The more examples it sees, the smarter it gets. * Example: Alexa, ChatGPT * Other AI learn from self-play: * The AI plays a game against itself a huge number of times * Eventually, it learns the patterns that allow it to win the game * Example: DeepBlue Chess Engine

### 1.6 Robots of the future ##### Artificial Intelligence The Three Laws of Robotics are a set of rules devised by science fiction author Isaac Asimov. * A robot may not injure a human being or, through inaction, allow a human being to come to harm. * A robot must obey orders given it by human beings except where such orders would conflict with the First Law. * A robot must protect its own existence as long as such protection does not conflict with the First or Second Law.

### 1.6 Robots of the future ##### Robot Vehicles ##### How Do Self-Driving Cars Work? * **Sensors and Cameras:** These help the car "see" the environment and detect obstacles. * **Computer Processor:** This is like the car's brain. It processes the information from the sensors and cameras, deciding how to navigate the roads safely. * **GPS:** GPS helps self-driving cars know their location and plan routes. * **Machine Learning:** The car learns from data to improve its driving over time. It can recognize patterns and make decisions based on past experiences.
### 1.6 Robots of the future ##### Robot Vehicles ##### Benefits of Self-Driving Cars * **Safety:** They can reduce accidents caused by human errors like distractions or fatigue. * **Accessibility:** They can help people who can't drive, like the elderly or those with disabilities, to get around. * **Efficiency:** They can reduce traffic jams by communicating with other cars and choosing the best routes.
### 1.6 Robots of the future ##### Robot Vehicles ##### Challenges of Self-Driving Cars * **Technology:** The technology is still being developed and needs to be perfect to ensure safety. * **Regulations:** Laws and regulations need to be updated to include self-driving cars. * **Ethical Decisions:** Programming cars to make tough decisions in dangerous situations is complex.
